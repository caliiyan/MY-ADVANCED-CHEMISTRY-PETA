Reflection

The Importance of Knowing the Chemical Composition: Understanding the chemical composition of everyday products helps us make informed choices about what we use and consume. Knowing about substances like fructose in Coca-Cola, lactose in Milk Loaded, and triclosan in Safeguard reveals their impact on our health and environment. By recognizing these ingredients, we become more conscious of their effects and can select products that align with our values on health, safety, and sustainability. This knowledge also deepens our appreciation of chemistry's role in daily life.

Why Manufacturers Don’t Use Real Chemical Names: Manufacturers avoid using complex chemical names on labels to make products more consumer-friendly. For example, “Triclosan (C₁₂H₇Cl₃O₂)” might seem confusing or intimidating, so “antibacterial agent” is used instead. Simplified labels help consumers feel more comfortable and confident in choosing products without being overwhelmed by scientific terms. This balance between clarity and transparency improves accessibility and marketability.
